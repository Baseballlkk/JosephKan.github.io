Projectile Motion

Analysis

  1. horizontal: constant velocity motion (since there is no force acting on the horizontal direction)
  2. vertical: constant acceleration motion (since there is only gravitational force acting on the object.)

Notice: Motion on different directions are independent.

Equations

$${x=v_0 cos\theta_0 \times t \brace y=v_0 sin\theta_0 \times t -\frac{1}{2} g t^2}$$

Projectile Motion Function

$$y=tan\theta_0 \cdot x-\frac{g}{2(v_0\cdot cos\theta_0)^2}\cdot x^2$$

This qwuation also shows that the projectile is a parabola.

proof of projectile motion function

By the equations of projectile motion, we can get: $$t=\frac{x}{v_0 \cdot cos\theta_0}$$

$$\Rightarrow y=tan\theta_0 \cdot x-\frac{g}{2(v_0\cdot cos\theta_0)^2}\cdot x^2$$

Uniform Circular Motion

Definition

The tangent velocity of the object is a constant and the track of the motion is a circle.

Centripetal Force

$$a=\frac{v^2}{R}=r\cdot \omega^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 R}{T^2}$$

Period

$$T=\frac{2\pi r}{v}$$
