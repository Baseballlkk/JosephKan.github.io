Motion

  1. Can occur three dimensions at the same time.
  2. Force can affect how an object's motion.
  3. if the size and shape won't affect our analysis, we can regard it as a particle.

Kinematic Variables

position
where the particle is.
displacement
$$\Delta \vec{x}=x_2-x_1$$
path length
the sum of whole of the path length the object move.
velocity
$$\vec{v}=\frac{\Delta \vec{x}}{\Delta t}$$
to solve instantaneous velocity:
$$lim_{\Delta t\rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta \vec{x}}{\Delta t}$$
speed
$$lim_{\Delta t\rightarrow 0} \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t}$$
acceleration
$$\vec{a}=\frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}$$
instantaneous acceleration: $$lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0}\frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}=\frac{dv}{dt}=\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}$$

Constant Acceleration Motion

  1. definition: The acceleration of a motion is a constant
  2. equations
    • $$v=v_0+at$$
    • $$x=x_0+v_0t+1/2at^2 $$
    • $$v^2=v_0^2+2aS$$

Vary Acceleration Motion

governing equations
