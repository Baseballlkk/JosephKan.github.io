linear or not

if the ODEs can be written in $$a_n(x)y^{(n)}+a_{n-1}(x)y^{(n-1)}+...+a_2(x)y''+a_1(x)y'+a_0(x)y=R(x)$$

then the ODEs are called to be linear equation.

Types of Solutions

General Solution

The solutions can fit in all the situation of the ODE.

Particular Solution

Particular solution can only fit in the ODE that R(x) is given.

Ways to Solve ODEs

Sparation of Variable

$$f(x)dx=g(y)dy$$

$$\Rightarrow \int f(x) dx=\int g(y) dy +C$$

Exact Differential Equaiton

if an ODE can be written in :$$M(x,y) dx+N(x,y)dy=0$$

and the $$M(x,y)=\frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial x} $$
$$ N(x,y)=\frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial y}$$

then the solution is:$$\Phi = \int M(x,y) dx+k(y) = \int N(x,y) dy+k(x)=c$$

1st order ODEs

if the ODE is $$y'+P(x)y=Q(x)$$

suppose$$I=e^{\int P(x)dx}$$

then $$I(y'+P(x)y)=Q(x)\cdot I$$

$$\Rightarrow (I\cdot y)'=Q(x)\cdot I$$

$$\Rightarrow y=I^{-1}\int Q(x)\cdot I dx$$

Bernoullis Equation

a Bernoullis equaiton must written: $$y'+P(x)y=Q(x)y^{\alpha}$$

suppose $$u=y^{1-\alpha}$$ to solve the equation.
